Chicken Pot Pie

A cozy and delicious meal for those days that are cold.

While this dish does take some time (really, what home cooking doesnt?), it's really worth trying out.

Think: Marie Caullenders, but at home!

Ingredients:
  • 6 Tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 Medium onion, diced
  • 3 Carrots, sliced
  • 2 Celery stalks, sliced
  • 3 Cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/3 Cup of flour
  • 3 large pinches of dried thyme
  • 1/2 Cup of heavy cream
  • 2 Cups chicken stock
  • 600 Grams (around 1.3 pounds) cooked chicken
  • 1 Cup of frozen peas
  • Coarse salt (optional)
  • 1 Egg, for egg wash
  • Pie dough, for top and bottom (DIY recipe can be found here.)
Steps:
  1. Slice celery, carrots, and onion; mince garlic.
  2. Shred 600g of cooked chicken (can use mixer to cut time).
  3. Bring a pot to medium heat and melt 6 tablespoons of unsalted butter.
  4. Once fully melted, add the sliced veggies and garlic. Season as needed with salt and pepper.
  5. Cook until onions are translucent, then add 1/3 cup of flour. Add herb of choice.
  6. Add 2 cups of chickeb broth and 1/2 cup of heavy cream. Scrape the bottom well.
  7. Bring toa simmer and cook on low until it starts to thicken (no longer than about 10 minute). Season if need.
  8. Add heaping cup of frozen peas, along with shredded chicken and stir to incorporate.
  9. Go ahead and preheat the oven to 400 degrees.
  10. Roll out your pie dough. The bottom should be a little wider for deeper dishes. Gently secure it into the dish, don't stretch. The top piece should be the circumfrence of the dish.
  11. Prepare an egg wash with one egg.
  12. Finalize salt and pepper chicken mixture.
  13. Fill the pie dish!
  14. Cover the dish with top portion of pie dough. Use egg wash as binding agent around the edges. Seal the bottom edges over.
  15. Cover the top of the pie with egg wash for a nice golden color.
  16. Sprinkle some salt course salt and pepper on top.
  17. Bake for around 45 minutes.
  18. Let it cool for around 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
